Answer this question:

How much money would you have in a year if you invested $110 at 10% interest?

First we calculated the interest payment for year 2. Recall that the interest rate is 10%. This time the principal is $110. To find the interest payment, we need to multiply the interest rate times the principal (Present Value) amount:

10% * $110 = $11

Now, add the interest payment to the initial investment (principal).

$110 + $11 = $121

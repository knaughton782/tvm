Answer this question:

How much money would you have in another year if you rolled over the $110?

Let's rephrase the question to a form you're already familiar with.

How much money would you have in a year if you invested $110 (calculated in the previous question) at 10% interest?

To figure out the answer:

  1. To find out how much we'll have after the 2nd year, we first need to calculate the interest payment for year 2. Recall that the interest rate is 10%. This time the principal is $110. To find the interest payment, multiply the interest rate times the principal (PV) amount.
    10% * $110 = $11
  2. Now, add the interest payment to the initial investment (principal).
    $110 + $11 = $121

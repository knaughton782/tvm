TVM: The Basics

Introduction

One of the central concepts of finance is that a dollar today is worth more than a dollar in the future.

Intuitively, you already understand that today's dollar is worth more than tomorrow's dollar. Transactions operate on this principle every day.

When you don't have enough cash to buy something, you may pay with a credit card; when the bill arrives, interest has been added to compensate the credit card company for lending you money.

Conversely, if you deposit money in a savings account, the bank pays you interest to compensate you for lending them money.

In review, we:

  • Calculated the 1st year of interest by multiplying the interest rate, 10% times the 1st year starting principle, $100.
    ($100 * 10%) = $10

  • Calculated the 2nd year of interest by finding 10% of the 2nd year starting principal, $110.
    ($110 * 10%) = $11

  • Added the 1st and 2nd year interest to the principal of $100.
    ($100 + $10 + $11) = $121

In other words, we calculated the interest earned in each individual year and added all the interest payments back to the principal to find the Future Value (FV).

Although, this method does produce the correct answer, what would you do if asked for the future value after 100 years?

You could create a spreadsheet of formulas with 100 interest payment calculations. However, its time consuming and fortunately, unncessary.

Instead, we'll derive a general equation that will allow you to solve for Future Value (FV), Present Value (PV), Interest rate (i), or number of years (n).

    (110 * 0.10) = 11
  3. Add the 1st and 2nd year interest to the principal of 100.

TVM: The Basics

Equation

Now its just a matter of simplifying the formula and grouping like variables.
[(PV * i)] + [(PV * i) + PV] * i + PV


  1. Start with the parenthesis by multiplying the variables within.
    PVi + [PVi + PV] * i + PV
  2. Distribute the multiplication to the variables within the middle bracket.
    PVi + PVi2 + PVi + PV
  3. Group like variables.
    PVi + PVi2 + PVi + PV → PVi2 + 2PVi + PV
  4. Use the distributive property to separate the like term. Here it is PV.
    PVi2 + 2PVi + PV → PV(i2 + 2i + 1)

TVM: The Basics

Equation


  • The equation in the parentheses can be simplified to (x + 1)(x + 1). You may have seen this equation in the math help under FOIL.

  • Think of (i + 1) as a number. A number multiplied by itself is also known as sqaring that number, which is what we will do here.

  • Remember, back to the problem: the term is 2 years. Therefore, n = 2 and we can replace the number in the exponent with the variable.

Adding FV to the other side of the equal sign, we arrive at:

FV = PV(1 + i)n


We could continue to derive the equation for 3, 10, 100 years. After a lot of algebraic manipulation, the outcome would be the same. Its not so important that you're able to repeat the derivation, but that you understand the equation can be derived from individual calculations.

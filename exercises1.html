If I deposit $2,000 in an account that earns an annually compounded rate of 8%, how much will my account be worth in 15 years?

Variables:

PV = 2000
FV = ?
i = 0.08
n = 15

Plug the numbers into the equation:

FV = PV(1 + i)n
FV = 2000(1 + 0.08)15
FV = 2000(1.08)15
FV = 2000(3.172169)
FV = $6,344.34

Solution

How much will $3,000 in a certificate of deposit that pays 3% annual interest be worth in 4 years?

PV = 3000
FV = ?
Future Value factor = 1.1255

To find the FV using the Future Value factor, multiply the Present Value by the Future Value factor:

FV = PV(Future Value Factor)
FV = 3000(1.1255)
FV = $3,376.50

TVM: The Basics

Introduction

TVM Equation

Time Value of Money(TVM) is the equation we use to convert between today's money and money in the future. Transactions operate on this principle every day.

Businesses use the equation to compare investments alternatives, to assist decision-makers by converting project opportunities into dollars, or to borrow money from lenders. Individuals use the TVM equation to plan for retirement savings or to compare the cost of a prospective mortgage or auto loan.

One of the central concepts of finance is that a dollar today is worth more than a dollar in the future. For example, if you make a purchase with a credit card, interest is added to the bill to compensate the credit card company for lending you money. Conversely, if you deposit money in a savings account, the bank pays you interest to compensate you for lending them money.

Introduction - Variables

What factors would you consider in determining how much Future Value (FV) the customer should pay at a later date?

Let's attach some variable names to these factors to understand them better. Please note: these colors will be used throughout the tutorials to help identify the variables.

Present Value (PV)

How much is being borrowed?
Present Value (PV) is the value in today's terms.

Future Value (FV)

How much will you have in the future?
Future Value (FV) is the value you will have in the future given a certain interest rate and/or time period.

Time (n)

How long before the customer pays?
Time (n) is the measure of time in years.

Interest (i)

What is the risk that the customer will not be able to pay the account at a later date? What else could your company do with the money if the customer paid now? If the loan is long enough, will inflation further erode the value of tomorrow's dollar? Interest (i) is the rate of return, compensating for risk and opportunity cost.

    2. First identify the interest rate and principle in the question. Then multiply the interest rate times the principal amount.

    10% x $100 = $10

  2. Finally, to calculate the amount you'd have in one year, add the interest payment back to the initial investment (principal).
    $100 + $10 = $110

Reciprocals

  • Find the reciprocal of a number by converting the number to a fraction, then switching the numerator and denominator. A number multiplied by its reciprocal equals 1. This makes reciprocals useful for canceling numbers and exponents.

TVM Equation

Now that we have variables in place of numbers, its just a matter of simplifying the formula and grouping like variables.

[(PV + i)] + [(PV * i) + PV] * i + PV

  1. Start with the parenthesis by multiplying the variables within.
    PVi + [PVi + PV] * i + PV
  2. Distribute the multiplication to the variables within the middle bracket.
    PVi + PVi2 + PVi + PV
  3. Group like variables.
    PVi + PVi2 + PVi + PV  ⟶  PVi2 + 2PVi + PV
  4. Use the distributive property to separate the like term, PV.
    PV i2 + 2PV i + PV  ⟶   PV (i2 + 2i + 1)
  5. The equation in the parentheses can be simplified to (x + 1)(x + 1). You can find information on this equation in the math help under FOIL.
    PV (i2 + 2i + 1)  ⟶   PV(i + 1)(i + 1)
  6. Think of (i + 1) as a number. A number multiplied by itself is that number squared.
    PV(i + 1)(i + 1)  ⟶   PV(i + 1)2
  7. Remember back to the problem: the term is 2 years. Therefore, n = 2. We can replace the number in the exponent with the variable.
    PV( i + 1) 2  ⟶   PV( i + 1) n

Help Me!

How many years will it take for you to earn $500 on your $1000 investment if you are guaranteed a 9% rate of return?

First, identify the variables in the problem.

PV = 1000      FV = 1500      i = 9% or 0.09      n = ?

Now solve for the unknown variable, in this case n. Remember PEMDAS.

  1. P: Add the numbers in the parentheses.
    1500 = 1000(1.09)n
  2. MD: Isolate the parentheses by dividing each side by 1000. Then convert the fraction on the left side of the equal sign to a decimal.
  3. E: Many people will get stuck here. To manipulate an unknown exponent, take the natural log (ln) of each side. ln is a button on your calculator. Pay attention to what happens to the exponent n.
    ln 1.5 = n(ln 1.09)
  4. MD: To isolate n, divide both sides by (ln 1.09). Then solve for n.

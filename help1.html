Answer this question:

How much money would you have in 1 year if you invested $100 at a rate of 10%?

To figure out how much we will have in 1 year, we need to calculate the interest payment and then add the interest payment back to the initial investment (principal). Here are some tips:

  • Interest (i) is a rate. Look for a percentage or decimal.
  • Time (n) is an expression of time or number of years.
  • Future Value (FV) and Present Value (PV): To distinguish between PV and FV, ask yourself whether the dollar amount is money you have now or in the future. Since the $100 is today's investment, it must be PV. The question asks what it will be worth, indicating we want to find FV.

To figure out the answer:

  1. Calculate the interest payment.

    2. First identify the interest rate and principle in the question. Then multiply the interest rate times the principal amount.

    How much money would you have in 1 year if you invested $100 at a rate of 10%?

    10% x $100 = $10

  2. Finally, to calculate the amount you'd have in one year, add the interest payment back to the initial investment (principal).
    $100 + $10 = $110

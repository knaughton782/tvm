Introduction

This is the first of four tutorials on the Time Value of Money. In this first part, we'll start with the basic equation and variables. Subsequent tutorials will add complexity by introducing compound interest, payments, and annuities.

TVM may require you to use some math skills that you haven’t used in a while. Contact your instructor if you need additional help.

By the end of this tutorial, you will:

  • Understand the relationship between variables in the TVM equation
  • Memorize and recall the general form of the TVM equation
  • Identify the TVM variables within a given word problem

TVM: The Basics

Introduction

One of the central concepts of finance is that a dollar today is worth more than a dollar in the future. Businesses and transactions operate on this principle every day.

For example, if you make a purchase with a credit card, interest is added to the bill to compensate the credit card company for lending you money. Conversely, if you deposit money in a savings account, the bank pays you interest to compensate you for lending them money.

Present Value (PV)

How much is being borrowed? Present Value (PV) is the value in today's terms.

Time (n)

How long before the customer pays? Time (n) is the measure of time in years.

Interest (i)

Interest (i) is the rate of return, compensating for risk and opportunity cost.

Help Me!

Follow the same pattern we used to solve our first problem.

How much would you pay today for a promise of $259.37 in 10 years at a suitable discount rate of 10%?

  • First, identify the variables in the problem:

    This time we’re looking for PV, because the question asks how much you would pay today.

    PV = ?

    We know that the $259.37 is the FV, because it will be paid in the future.

    FV = $259.37

    Remember that i is a percentage or decimal.

    i = 10% or .10

    n is an expression of time such as years or decades.

    n = 10

  • Once you've identified the variables, plug the numbers into the equation:

    FV = PV(1 + i) n   ⟶   $259.37 = PV(1 + 0.10) 10
  • Then solve for the unknown variable, in this case PV. Remember to use the order of operations (PEMDAS).
    • Parentheses: Add the numbers in the parentheses.
      $259.37 = PV(1.10) 10
    • Exponents: Raise the number in the parentheses to the exponent. Leave enough significant digits so that you don’t commit a rounding error.
      $259.37 = PV(2.5937)
    • Multiple and Divide: Divide both sides by the same number to isolate PV.
    • Adding and Subtracting is not necessary for this problem. The addition in this specific equation was taken care of in the parentheses.
  • Finally, simplify the fraction to solve for PV.
